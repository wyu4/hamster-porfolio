Hamsters

Imagine coming home to a tiny, fluffy ball of pure joy zooming around its enclosure like a tiny NASCAR champion!

Hamsters are the perfect low-maintenance pets that fit right into your life, no matter how busy you are. They're ridiculously adorable, with those chubby cheeks and twitchy little noses, and their playful antics will keep you endlessly entertained! Whether they're stuffing their cheeks with snacks or running on their little wheel like they're training for the Olympics, hamsters are guaranteed to steal your heart.

Species

Here are different kinds of hamsters!

Dwarf Roborovski

The Dwarf Roborovski is a nocturnal hamster who grows up to two inches long, and only weights an ounce. Although most of them don't particularly enjoy being around humans, they tend to get along with other Robos.

Syrian / Golden

The Syrian hamster (sometimes known as the Golden hamster) is the largest and most common breed of hamster in the household. Syrians can weigh nearly five times as much as any other breed. Their size makes them easier to handle, which makes Syrians better suited for new hamster owners. However, Syrians are much more territorial, meaning they should be kept alone rather than with other hamsters.

Winter White Dwarf

The Winter white dwarf hamster (also known as the Russian dwarf hamster) are way smaller than Syrian hamsters, but they make up for it by being super friendly and easy to tame. Plus, they're really chill with both people and other winter white hamsters, which makes them a top pick for both newbie and experienced hamster owners.

Chinese

The Chinese hamster might be small, but it definitely stand out with their super cute long tails and a black stripe running down their backs. Just like other hamsters, they're pretty chill and don't need a ton of maintenance. Even though they're tiny, they still need a spacious setup and lots of chew toys to keep their teeth and little legs in good shape.

Life of A Hamster

What are they up to and how do we care for them?

Sleep Cycle

Hamsters are crepuscular and nocturnal. This means that they'll spend most of the day dozing off. However, they'll be quite active at night! hamsters sleep for around a total of 12 hours per day.

Food

Hamsters eat a variety of bugs, fruits, vegetables, and seeds. Be sure to feed them every day. Each hamster will have different portions. Make sure to not overfeed them, it's no good!

Enclosure

A hamster needs a house! Despite their small size, hamsters need a lot of space to run around and explore! Make sure to do your research and get them a big enclosure!

Bedding

These little buddies are known to burrow. This means that they will need A LOT of bedding to create small tunnels. Some will require up to eight inches of bedding! It seems like a lot, but they'll love it.

Handling

Hamsters are very small and delicate animals. Humans on the other hand are huge, and can pose a threat. So naturally, hamsters tend to be very afraid of humans... If they don't open up to you right away, be patient! Some hamsters take up to weeks, or even months to be tamed! Be ready to be an acute observer in their lives!

Entertainment

Hamsters can become bored! After all, there's only so much space in an enclosure! Be sure to get them a well-sized hamster wheel, perhaps a sand bath, toys, and LOTS of treats. If your hamster grows accustomed to you, you could also take them out of the enclosure and put them in a hamster-safe room for free-roaming.
